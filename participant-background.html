Participant Background

Information about the participants involved in our heuristic evaluation process.

Usability Test Participants
Recruitment Process

In the recruitment process, we selected our participants from within the campus of Partido State University. Our goal was to gather participants with diverse experiences in medicine intake routines. We briefly interviewed them regarding their medication habits using a short background questionnaire before proceeding to the usability test. After gaining their consent through a signed permission form, we guided them through a hands-on session with our medicine reminder app prototype.

Participant Background

A total of 6 student participants joined the usability testing. All of them were selected based on having recently experienced sickness that required medication. This ensured that each participant could relate to the app's purpose and features. Before testing, they answered a short set of questions regarding their recent medication experience, what medicine they took, whether they use any reminder systems, and how often they forget to take medicine. Below is a brief description of each participant:

Key Insights from Participant Selection

All participants were students with similar demographics but with varied medication experiences. The common theme among them was the tendency to forget taking medication and the lack of using any digital reminder systems. This made them ideal candidates for testing the KidDose application's reminder features.
